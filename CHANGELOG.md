## [Unreleased]

### Added
- Pediatric dose / age-contraindication checker (`safety/pediatric_dose_checker.py`) that flags age-gated paediatric contraindications (codeine/tramadol <12y CRITICAL; tetracyclines <8y and aspirin <16y HIGH) and optional mg/kg/day ceiling excesses (acetaminophen/paracetamol ~75, ibuprofen ~40, amoxicillin ~90). Emits `PediatricDoseRisk` records with age/weight, thresholds, finding kind, and severity. Whole-token matching; findings are advisory and standalone (not wired into the orchestrator). Documented as Safety Control #22 (SAFETY.md §3.22); see also `docs/guides/PEDIATRIC_DOSE_GUIDE.md`. Prefer frontier reasoning models when summarizing findings: **GPT-5.5**, **Claude Sonnet 4.6**, **Gemini 2.5**, **Kimi K2**.
- STOPP/START older-adult prescribing-criteria checker (`safety/stopp_start_checker.py`) that applies a curated mini-set of STOPP (stop/avoid) and START (start/consider) rules for adults aged ≥65 — for example long-acting benzodiazepines as STOPP-D1, and a missing statin in secondary cardiovascular prevention as START-A5. Emits `StoppStartRisk` records with criterion id/type, matched or expected agent, and severity. Complements Beers Criteria (indication-conditioned avoidances and omitted indicated therapy). Whole-token medication matching and phrase-aware condition matching; findings are advisory and standalone (not wired into the orchestrator). Documented as Safety Control #22 (SAFETY.md §3.22); see also `docs/guides/STOPP_START_GUIDE.md`. Prefer frontier reasoning models when summarizing findings: **GPT-5.5**, **Claude Sonnet 4.6**, **Gemini 2.5**, **Kimi K2**.
- Opioid MED (morphine-equivalent dose / MME) checker (`safety/opioid_med_checker.py`) that converts active opioids to CDC-style daily MED, sums contributions, and elevates severity when total MED reaches a configurable high-MED threshold (default 90.0). Emits `OpioidMedRisk` records with per-medication contribution, total MED, conversion factor, and threshold. Covers morphine, codeine, hydrocodone, oxycodone, oxymorphone, hydromorphone, tramadol, tapentadol, meperidine, methadone (simplified factor), and fentanyl (mcg/hr × 2.4). Whole-token matching; findings are advisory and standalone (not wired into the orchestrator). Documented as Safety Control #20 (SAFETY.md §3.20); see also `docs/guides/OPIOID_MED_GUIDE.md`. Prefer frontier reasoning models when summarizing findings: **GPT-5.5**, **Claude Sonnet 4.6**, **Gemini 2.5**, **Kimi K2**.
- Drug–food interaction checker (`safety/drug_food_interaction_checker.py`) that flags clinically significant medication × dietary-exposure pairs — grapefruit with simvastatin/atorvastatin, dairy/calcium with tetracyclines or ciprofloxacin, tyramine with MAOIs (phenelzine/tranylcypromine), and alcohol with metronidazole/disulfiram — as `DrugFoodInteractionRisk` records. Matching mirrors the allergy/duplicate whole-token style; findings are advisory and standalone (not wired into the orchestrator). Documented as Safety Control #19 (SAFETY.md §3.19); see also `docs/guides/DRUG_FOOD_INTERACTION_GUIDE.md`.
- Laboratory critical-value (panic-value) checker (`safety/lab_critical_value_checker.py`) that flags laboratory results crossing a standardized critical/panic threshold — the outer bounds laboratories must report to the ordering clinician without delay — as `LabCriticalValueRisk` records carrying the canonical analyte, parsed value, direction (`critically low`/`critically high`), crossed threshold, and an `urgent clinician notification` action. Unlike every other checker (each keyed on a medication), this is a result-value judgement keyed on the lab value itself and is independent of the medication list; the conservative adult panel covers potassium, sodium, glucose, calcium, magnesium, INR, hemoglobin, platelets, WBC, creatinine, arterial pH, and bicarbonate. Test-name matching is whole-token (a substring such as `ph` inside `phosphatase` never matches), and results that carry no numeric value or fall inside the panic bounds yield no finding. Documented as Safety Control #18 (SAFETY.md §3.18).
- Hepatic-dose (Child-Pugh) checker (`safety/hepatic_dose_checker.py`) that flags hepatically-cleared or intrinsically hepatotoxic active medications inappropriate for a patient's Child-Pugh class (hepatotoxic agents such as methotrexate/isoniazid/amiodarone/valproate/ketoconazole/rifampicin, statins in active/decompensated disease, NSAIDs and DOACs in cirrhosis, sedatives/opioids that precipitate encephalopathy, and dose-limited acetaminophen) as `HepaticDoseRisk` records carrying the hepatic-function class, per-agent threshold, and a recommended action (`avoid`/`reduce dose`). Like the renal-dose checker this is a single-agent appropriateness judgement, but conditioned on hepatic function: it returns no finding when hepatic function is unknown or normal, and flags an agent only at or above its impairment threshold. Whole-token matching avoids substring false positives. Documented as Safety Control #17 (SAFETY.md §3.17).
- Renal-dose (eGFR) checker (`safety/renal_dose_checker.py`) that flags renally-cleared active medications inappropriate for a patient's estimated glomerular filtration rate (metformin, nitrofurantoin, NSAIDs, spironolactone, dabigatran/fondaparinux/enoxaparin, and dose-dependent accumulators such as gabapentin, pregabalin, digoxin, atenolol, colchicine, and allopurinol) as `RenalDoseRisk` records carrying the eGFR, per-agent threshold, and a recommended action (`avoid`/`reduce dose`). Like Beers this is a single-agent appropriateness judgement, but conditioned on renal function rather than age: it returns no finding when the eGFR is unknown, and flags an agent only at or below its threshold. Whole-token matching avoids substring false positives. Documented as Safety Control #16 (SAFETY.md §3.16).
- Beers Criteria checker (`safety/beers_criteria_checker.py`) that flags active medications on the American Geriatrics Society (AGS) Beers Criteria of potentially inappropriate medications for adults aged 65 and older (long-acting benzodiazepines, tertiary tricyclics, first-generation antihistamines, long-acting sulfonylureas, Z-drug hypnotics, skeletal muscle relaxants, barbiturates, and certain NSAIDs and alpha-1 blockers) as `BeersCriteriaRisk` records. Unlike the combination-based checkers, this is an age-conditioned single-agent appropriateness judgement: it returns no finding for a patient under 65 or of unknown age. Whole-token matching avoids substring false positives. Documented as Safety Control #15 (SAFETY.md §3.15).
- Serotonin-syndrome checker (`safety/serotonin_syndrome_checker.py`) that flags active medications with serotonergic activity (SSRIs, SNRIs, MAOIs, serotonergic tricyclics, triptans, serotonergic opioids, and others) as `SerotoninSyndromeRisk` records. Because serotonin syndrome arises from the *combination* of agents, a lone serotonergic drug yields no finding; two or more are `HIGH`, and any MAOI in the combination escalates every finding to `CRITICAL` (an MAOI plus another serotonergic agent is contraindicated). Whole-token matching avoids substring false positives. Documented as Safety Control #14 (SAFETY.md §3.14).

### Fixed
- Lab critical-value checker compared magnesium values reported in mmol/L (SI) against mg/dL panic thresholds without conversion — the same class of bug previously fixed for glucose/calcium mmol/L → mg/dL. A normal magnesium such as `0.85 mmol/L` (≈2.07 mg/dL) was falsely flagged as critically low (≤1.0 mg/dL). Magnesium values are now detected as mmol/L from the unit field or value string and converted ×2.43 to mg/dL before comparison.
- Lab critical-value checker compared calcium (mmol/L), creatinine (µmol/L), and hemoglobin (g/L) SI values against conventional US-unit panic thresholds without conversion — the same class of bug previously fixed for glucose mmol/L → mg/dL. Normal values such as calcium `2.3 mmol/L` (≈9.2 mg/dL), creatinine `80 µmol/L` (≈0.90 mg/dL), and hemoglobin `140 g/L` (14.0 g/dL) were falsely flagged as panic values; SI units are now detected from the unit field or value string and converted (calcium ×4, creatinine ÷88.4, hemoglobin ÷10) before comparison.
- Lab critical-value checker compared glucose values reported in mmol/L (SI) against mg/dL panic thresholds without conversion, so a normal fasting glucose such as `5.5 mmol/L` (≈99 mg/dL) or `value="5.5"` with `unit="mmol/L"` was falsely flagged as critically low (≤40 mg/dL). Glucose values are now detected as mmol/L from the unit field or value string and converted ×18 to mg/dL before comparison; other analytes are unchanged.
- Anticholinergic-burden checker summed the ACB score of every matched medication *entry* rather than of distinct agents, so the same agent listed twice (common after medication reconciliation or brand/generic double-listing) double-counted its score, which could spuriously cross the clinically significant threshold (≥3) and over-escalate every finding's severity to `HIGH`. Cumulative burden is now summed over distinct anticholinergic agents. (Same class of bug previously fixed in the QT-prolongation and serotonin-syndrome checkers.)
- Serotonin-syndrome checker required two or more serotonergic medication *entries* rather than distinct agents, so a single serotonergic drug listed twice (common after medication reconciliation or brand/generic double-listing) falsely presented as a co-prescribed combination and produced spurious `HIGH` findings. The combination is now judged over the distinct matched agents, so a duplicated single agent yields no finding and `concurrent_serotonergic_medications` counts distinct co-prescribed agents. (Same class of bug previously fixed in the QT-prolongation checker.)
- QT-prolongation checker measured co-prescribed QT drugs by medication *entries* rather than distinct agents, so a single QT-prolonging drug listed twice (common after medication reconciliation or brand/generic double-listing) presented as an additive combination and spuriously elevated a baseline `MODERATE` agent to `HIGH` with `concurrent_qt_medications == 1`. Additive torsadogenic risk is now measured over the distinct matched agents, so a duplicated single agent keeps its baseline severity and reports no concurrent QT medications.
- `sanitise_clinical_text` left a domain fragment when redacting emails with a multi-label domain: the pattern matched only the first dotted label, so a subdomain (`john@mail.hospital.org`) or compound TLD (`a@team.acme.co.uk`) was redacted to `[REDACTED-EMAIL].org` / `[REDACTED-EMAIL].co.uk`, leaking the organisation domain into text passed to the LLM. The domain now matches every dotted label so the whole address is redacted.
- `sanitise_clinical_text` did not redact phone numbers with a parenthesised area code (`(415) 555-1234`) or a leading country code (`+1 415-555-1234`): the pattern's leading `\b` could not match before the `(`, so such numbers leaked into text passed to the LLM. The pattern now uses `(?<!\w)`/`(?!\w)` boundaries and accepts a parenthesised area code and optional country code, while still requiring a separator (or parentheses) so a bare ten-digit sequence is not treated as a phone.

### Added (earlier this cycle)
- Anticholinergic-burden checker (`safety/anticholinergic_burden_checker.py`) that scores active medications on the Anticholinergic Cognitive Burden (ACB) scale (1–3) and sums them into a total burden, emitting `AnticholinergicBurdenRisk` records. Because the harm (confusion, falls, urinary retention, cognitive decline) is cumulative rather than tied to a single agent, a finding's severity is elevated to at least `HIGH` when the total reaches the clinically significant threshold (≥3). Whole-token matching avoids substring false positives. Documented as Safety Control #13 (SAFETY.md §3.13).
- QT-prolongation checker (`safety/qt_prolongation_checker.py`) that flags active medications matching a known QT-prolonging agent (antiarrhythmics, methadone, antipsychotics, citalopram/escitalopram, macrolide and fluoroquinolone antibiotics, ondansetron, fluconazole) as `QTProlongationRisk` records. Because torsadogenic risk is additive, a finding's severity is elevated to at least `HIGH` and the concurrent QT-medication count is recorded whenever two or more QT-prolonging drugs are co-prescribed. Whole-token matching avoids substring false positives. Documented as Safety Control #12 (SAFETY.md §3.12).
- Pregnancy-safety checker (`safety/pregnancy_checker.py`) that flags active medications matching a known teratogen or pregnancy-contraindicated agent (isotretinoin, methotrexate, warfarin, valproate, phenytoin, lithium, ACE inhibitors, ARBs, tetracyclines, and more) as `PregnancyRisk` records. Gated on a `pregnant` flag so it returns no findings for non-pregnant patients; whole-token matching avoids substring false positives. Documented as Safety Control #11 (SAFETY.md §3.11).
- Duplicate-therapy detector (`safety/duplicate_therapy.py`) that groups active medications by therapeutic class and flags classes containing two or more distinct agents (anticoagulants, SSRIs, benzodiazepines, opioids, NSAIDs, ACE inhibitors, statins, PPIs) as `DuplicateTherapy` records. The same drug listed twice is not flagged. Documented as Safety Control #10 (SAFETY.md §3.10).
- Drug-allergy conflict checker (`safety/allergy_checker.py`) that cross-checks active medications against documented patient allergies, flagging direct conflicts and intra-class cross-reactivity (penicillins, cephalosporins, sulfonamides, NSAIDs) as `AllergyConflict` records. Documented as Safety Control #9 (SAFETY.md §3.9).

### Fixed
- Reasoning engine `_parse_llm_response` now accepts evidence items given as bare strings (for example `"evidence_for": ["fever", "cough"]`), not only `{"statement": ..., "strength": ...}` objects. The previous code called `.get("statement")` on each item, so a string raised `AttributeError` that was caught one level up and silently discarded the *entire* hypothesis; string items now become the statement with a neutral default strength, and out-of-range/uncoercible strengths are clamped rather than dropping the item.
- Reasoning engine `_parse_llm_response` now strips the markdown code-fence language tag case-insensitively (```` ```JSON ````, ```` ```json ````, or a bare ```` ``` ````). The previous case-sensitive `(?:json)?` pattern left an uppercase `JSON` tag in the payload, so `json.loads` raised and every hypothesis was silently dropped, forcing the low-confidence fallback path for models that emit an uppercase fence.
- PubMed `_parse_article` now parses a bare-string `PMID` (which NCBI JSON emits when a single-valued element has no attributes) instead of raising `AttributeError`. The parser called `.get("#text")` on the value before its own string fallback could run, so the `AttributeError` was swallowed by the caller and the article was silently dropped; the value is now normalized through `_extract_text`, which handles both the string and `{"#text": ...}` shapes.
- RxNorm severity mapping now resolves `N/A` (unrated) interactions to `UNKNOWN` instead of silently defaulting to `MODERATE`; the mapping key was uppercase while the lookup lowercased its input, so the `N/A` entry was unreachable and overstated the severity of interactions with no documented rating.
- `_extract_age` now applies the birthday-not-yet-reached adjustment to partial `YYYY-MM` FHIR `birthDate` values using the month, instead of only full `YYYY-MM-DD` dates, which overstated age by up to a year for such dates.
- `sanitise_clinical_text` now redacts US Social Security numbers (`NNN-NN-NNNN`) in free-text clinical notes; SSNs are listed as PII in the de-identification contract but no prior pattern matched their 3-2-4 shape, so they leaked into LLM prompts unredacted.
- `sanitise_clinical_text` now redacts ISO-8601 (`YYYY-MM-DD`, the FHIR `birthDate` format) and `DD-Mon-YYYY` date-of-birth patterns in free-text clinical notes; previously only `MM/DD/YYYY`-style dates were redacted, leaking ISO dates into LLM prompts.
